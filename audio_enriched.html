What is a Diode?

A diode is a semiconductor device that allows current to flow in only one direction. It acts like a one-way street for electrical current. Diodes are crucial components in electronics, used for rectifying signals, protecting circuits, and more.

Types of Diodes

  • 1. PN Junction Diode: The most common type. Allows current only in forward bias.
  • 2. Zener Diode: Designed to allow current in reverse bias once a specific voltage is reached. Used in voltage regulation.
  • 3. Light Emitting Diode (LED): Emits light when forward biased. Used in displays and lighting.
  • 4. Schottky Diode: Known for fast switching and low voltage drop. Used in high-speed circuits.
  • 5. Photodiode: Converts light into current. Used in solar cells and light sensors.
  • 6. Tunnel Diode: Has negative resistance properties. Used in oscillators and amplifiers.
  • 7. Varactor Diode: Acts as a variable capacitor when reverse biased. Used in tuning circuits.

Diodes play an essential role in modern electronics by managing how current flows and protecting sensitive components from damage.
